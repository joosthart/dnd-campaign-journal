## Characters Present

- **Erophin** — A 150-year-old wood elf with golden eyes, green splashes, and two large horns. Dressed in black and brown broken leather, carrying an elegant black sword with an emerald and a longbow. Birds constantly flock around him. He followed his instincts (and his birds) to the carnival, sensing something lost but unable to name it.
- **Pip Moonstep** — A 22-year-old haregon (rabbit-folk), light brown skin, dark hair, very light blue eyes, carrying a short sword. Visited the Witchlight Carnival eight years ago as a young rabbit and lost something precious — his rhythm, the magical feeling that guided his blade-singing. He returned to find answers.
- **Baba (Uma)** — A tabaxi with purple skin and white hair, roughly 9 years old (though time is fuzzy for her). She works at the carnival doing odd jobs — lifting things, performing acrobatics — in exchange for housing. She doesn't quite remember how long she's been there. She received a free ticket from an anonymous benefactor.
- **Aruni** — A 142-year-old druid with light dreadlocks and a well-worn walking staff, carrying a big backpack full of plants and potions. She rarely visits cities, preferring nature. She is searching for someone but doesn't know who — only that small signs keep pointing her forward.

## Recap

Four strangers, each carrying the weight of something missing, converged on the Witchlight Carnival — a place of impossible wonder that arrives once every eight years. What began as an evening of cupcakes, swan rides, and balloon animals slowly unraveled into something far more troubling: a conspiracy of disappearances, stolen voices, and mirrors that lead to other worlds.

## Narrative Summary

### Arrival at the Carnival

The Witchlight Carnival announced itself in the way such things do — with purple-tinted air, enormous dragonflies, fairies trailing smoke and blinkies, and a low mist crawling across the ground. Through the great flower arch, the world became impossibly more colorful.

Pip stood before the entrance, memories of his last visit — eight years ago — pressing against him like a half-remembered dream. Erophin's birds, normally content to roost at nightfall, had instead pulled him here with unusual urgency, fluttering insistently toward the carnival grounds. Aruni followed the same avian trail, her own flock leading her to the elf they seemed so keen on. And Baba was already there, as she had been for... well, she wasn't entirely sure how long.

At the ticket booth, an elderly goblin processed the newcomers. Erophin attempted to pass as a child for a cheaper ticket — unconvincingly. Baba, it turned out, had a free ticket waiting from an anonymous benefactor, which she graciously shared with others. Everyone received fairy wings as proof of entry, and with that, the carnival opened its arms.

### First Encounters and the Feasting Orchard

The group drifted together with the casual inevitability of people who share unspoken common ground. Pip and Baba's first exchange was characteristically blunt — Baba demanding to know why everyone was there, Pip cautiously admitting something had changed the last time he visited. Aruni introduced herself with warmth. Erophin offered a dry politeness.

Passing the Calliope — a brightly painted wagon producing golden puffs of smoke — a monkey in a button-covered cloak requested a spare button. Erophin obliged, pulling one from his boot, earning jolly music in return. Baba was unimpressed. "Why would you do that? It's such ugly music."

In the Feasting Orchard, the air smelled of flowers and berry pie as elves on stilts picked fruit overhead. The group entered a cupcake eating contest: Aruni demolished the competition with druidic constitution and won a mysterious cupcake that was, in truth, a potion of invisibility. She kept this secret from the others — for now. A natural 20 deception check sealed the deal. Meanwhile, Pip noticed a female gnome playing music in a pear tree. She claimed to have been "traveling for a long time" and to have "seen many places," but offered nothing more.

### The Swan Gondola and Feathereen's Philosophy

The group boarded a flower-draped gondola piloted by Feathereen, a philosophical swan who opened with the question: "What is joy?" Pip answered with dancing and music. Erophin cited running through woods. Aruni spoke of birdsong and quiet sunny days. The ride circled the carnival's perimeter through dense banks of mist, taking about an hour, during which ages were exchanged — Pip 22, Aruni 142, Erophin 150, Baba refusing at first and then casually dropping "nine." Feathereen mused on whether time was an illusion, and Pip replied it was relative. "A wise thing to say for a young person," Feathereen noted. "I had a great teacher," Pip said quietly.

On the ride, Baba mentioned knowing the Hall of Illusions and a mime called Candlefoot — a monochromatic figure who had lost his voice when trying to propose to Palasha the mermaid. A raven had stolen it. Baba's assessment was characteristically blunt: "I think he's a coward."

### The Hall of Illusions

The Hall of Illusions was dominated by Tasha's cabinet — a glass case housing a wooden puppet of a grinning young woman in witch's clothing, identified as Tasha the Wizard, known for her Hideous Laughter spell. Erophin recognized the name: Tasha, also known as Iggwilv the Witch Queen, one of the most legendary archmages. He deduced that anyone approaching the cabinet would be hit with uncontrollable laughter.

Sure enough, they witnessed it firsthand: a halfling named Ruben, mid-proposal to his partner Ween, watched in horror as Ween collapsed into hideous cackling from the cabinet's magic. Ruben bolted into the hall. The party split up — Pip and Erophin pursued Ruben while Aruni stayed with the distraught Ween.

They found Ruben sitting before a mirror, where he'd been speaking with a mysterious small woman wearing a large face-mask who had asked him about his regrets and offered to help — then vanished the moment the others approached. Pip coaxed Ruben out ("She didn't mean it, man. She was bewitched."), and the reunion outside was met with cheers and a successful second proposal attempt.

The mirrors themselves showed visitors aging or growing younger depending on distance. Pip saw himself old — ears drooping, fur greying, eyes dulling. Erophin saw himself grey-haired but at peace, with something small and indistinct sitting on his shoulder. Baba glimpsed herself as a tiny baby kitten. "So small, so cute. So fast."

They also met **Candlefoot the Mime** — grey-skinned, colorless clothes, even his butterfly wings translucent — silently sharing flowers with passersby. Through pantomime, he confirmed that a black bird (a raven) had stolen his voice while he was proposing to Palasha. On a return visit, Erophin was hit by Tasha's Hideous Laughter near the cabinet, bursting into uncontrollable cackling before Pip pulled him away.

### The Big Top Extravaganza

Mr. Light, the flamboyant elven ringmaster, hosted the show from a spinning hoop above the ring, wielding a scepter with rotating ornaments. After a spectacular ten-minute opening act of fire-spitters, contortionists, and flying fairies, he called for volunteers.

Pip stepped up to dance. It did not go well. His rhythm — the very thing he'd lost — betrayed him. The crowd's enthusiasm faded to polite silence, then a single lingering clap. Baba followed with an acrobatic leap through a flaming hoop that the crowd was certain she wouldn't make — and stuck the landing to thunderous applause. Erophin attempted to shoot an arrow through a spectator's tall hat, but Mr. Light materialized beside him and snatched the arrow mid-flight. "We don't intend to harm those who go to the festival." Erophin pivoted to a whistling performance with his birds. It was, charitably, not his finest moment.

### Meeting Management

After the show, a bugbear named **Burly** — wearing a garden dress and a jack-o'-lantern helmet — appeared. "Management wants to see you." The party was led through a bramblewood wall into the staff area, past a clown named **Fecko** smoking a bubble pipe, and into a wagon where **Mr. Light** and **Mr. Witch** waited.

Mr. Light, all grins and grandeur, explained that his staff (the scepter) could sense every emotional nuance in the carnival — and the party had brought great spirits. When pressed about Pip's loss, Mr. Light offered careful deflection: "We are not responsible for such losses. Forces are at work on the other side, entirely beyond our control." Mr. Witch, sturdy and taciturn, checked his pocket watch and added flatly: "What is lost is lost. Gone's gone."

The clown Fecko crafted magical balloon animals for each of them — a bird for Erophin, a squirrel for Aruni, a rabbit for Pip, a cat for Baba — that floated impossibly in place.

### Burly's Confession

Once clear of the bosses, Burly pulled the group aside. He removed his helmet and dropped the act. People had been going missing. Things had been disappearing. Over multiple carnivals now. He believed Mr. Light and Mr. Witch knew more but that "somehow their hands are tied." He wanted the party to steal Mr. Witch's pocket watch — the device that keeps the carnival running on time — and use it as leverage to force answers.

The plan: the party would spread joy to earn a nomination for the Crowning of the Witchlight Monarch, creating a distraction. Meanwhile, someone would steal the watch backstage. Burly recommended three potential allies: Candlefoot, Dirlagraun, and Palasha. He also warned that the watch was attached to Mr. Witch's coat by a chain — they'd need something to cut it.

### Gathering Allies

**Candlefoot** agreed to find a cutting tool for the chain. Communication was laborious but successful through pantomime.

**Dirlagraun** — a displacer beast stationed at the Lost Property wagon, midnight blue with tentacles and fake butterfly wings — cared for lost children, dispensing sweet apple juice from a barrel around his neck. Mid-conversation, a boy named Viro snatched Dirlagraun's most treasured possession: a mirrored ball that had been the favorite toy of his missing child. Pip sprinted after the boy.

Dirlagraun's story was heartbreaking in its simplicity. His child had vanished from the carnival years ago. Without a trace. He'd seen a blackbird and suspected a connection. The party promised to help, and Dirlagraun gave them the mirrored ball. Pip eventually found Viro at a candy stall and recovered the boy, earning Dirlagraun's gratitude.

Aruni then revealed her secret: the cupcake was actually a potion of invisibility. "Why wouldn't you tell us in the first place?" The group decided to save it for the heist rather than reconnaissance.

### Spying and the Kenku

The party split. Baba, leveraging her staff access, led Erophin into the staff area to eavesdrop on Mr. Light and Mr. Witch's wagon. They overheard a heated exchange:

Mr. Witch: *"I'll calm down when that Kenku pest is gone."*
Mr. Light: *"She knows nothing. She'll get frustrated soon enough and be on her way."*

Meanwhile, at Silversong Lake, Pip watched Palasha the mermaid perform — until heckling from the crowd drove her to tears and she swam away. Pip tried to comfort her, learning she was friends with Candlefoot but gaining little new information. Erophin spotted the heckler shapeshifting from human form into a **Kenku** — a raven-like humanoid — and the chase was on.

The party cornered the Kenku, who identified herself as **Kettlesteam**. She was a warlock who had made a pact with an archfey named **Zybilna** — the ruler of a realm called **Prismeer** — but could no longer contact her patron. She had stolen Candlefoot's voice (held in a corn-husk doll with a thorny rose stem tied around its neck) not out of malice but to cause enough trouble to force Mr. Light and Mr. Witch to acknowledge her. She wanted answers about what was happening in Prismeer.

She offered a deal: help her get an audience with the carnival's owners, and she would return Candlefoot's voice. The party agreed, though trust was thin. Kettlesteam also shared something she'd overheard, speaking in Mr. Light's stolen voice:

*"Someone is going to find out about this. They'll shut us down."*
And Mr. Witch: *"We agreed to this pact. Our hands were forced. But her eyes were open. We let the Hourglass Coven take what it wants, and in return we stay in business."*

### The Crowning and the Heist

The plan came together. Candlefoot provided wire snips. Aruni ate the invisibility cupcake. Baba slipped backstage. The others took seats in the Big Top.

Mr. Light's golden scepter swept through the audience, seeking the soul who had brought the most joy to the carnival. The light settled on **Erophin** — the button-giver, the Ruben-rescuer, the generous elf whose good deeds had quietly accumulated all evening. He was crowned the Witchlight Monarch, showered in fairy dust and pixie confetti, and granted the Charm of the Monarch: the ability to sprout butterfly wings and fly, with enhanced charisma, usable three times.

While the crowd celebrated, Kettlesteam struck backstage — hurling a sticky goop over Mr. Witch as a distraction. The invisible Baba moved in, snipped the chain with Candlefoot's wire cutters, and pocketed the watch. A natural 20 on sleight of hand. Clean as a whistle.

### Through the Mirror

The carnival wound down. Mr. Light and Mr. Witch, unsurprisingly, noticed their missing watch and summoned the party to the Hall of Illusions. But instead of fury, they offered a door.

Looking into the aging mirrors, the party glimpsed Mr. Light and Mr. Witch's true reflections — younger versions revealed as **shadar-kai**, shadow elves from the Shadowfell.

Mr. Light explained: the land beyond the mirrors was called **Prismeer**. An archfey's domain. Anyone who sneaks into the carnival without a ticket is robbed of something precious, and whatever is stolen always finds its way to Prismeer. The mirror was a one-way portal. Getting back would be up to them.

Kettlesteam honored her deal — she removed the thorn from the corn-husk doll and returned Candlefoot's voice. There were tears. There was joy.

Mr. Light taught them the password: *"Hither, thither, here and there. Wander yonder, show me where."*

Before the final member stepped through, Mr. Witch offered a cryptic parting instruction: **"Mind the rule of three."** And Mr. Light added: **"Find the alicorn. And free the dormant queen at last."**

One by one, reflections reached out from the glass, touched them, and pulled them through.

Baba first. Then Pip. Then Aruni. And finally Erophin, newly crowned and freshly winged, stepped into the mist — and into Prismeer.

## Key Discoveries & Decisions

- Each party member has lost something, likely stolen during a visit to the carnival: Pip lost his rhythm, Erophin lost something he can't identify, Baba may have lost memories or her sense of time, and Aruni lost knowledge of who she's searching for.
- Mr. Light and Mr. Witch are secretly **shadar-kai** (shadow elves) whose true nature is visible in the Hall of Illusions' mirrors.
- The carnival operates under a **pact**: they let the "Hourglass Coven" take what it wants, and in return the carnival stays in business. Mr. Light and Mr. Witch claim their hands were forced.
- **Kettlesteam** the kenku warlock made a pact with an archfey named **Zybilna**, ruler of **Prismeer**, but has lost contact with her patron.
- The party stole **Mr. Witch's pocket watch** and used it as leverage to gain passage through the mirror portal to Prismeer.
- A mysterious **small woman with a large face/mask** appeared in the Hall of Illusions, asking about regrets and offering help, then vanishing.
- A **blackbird/raven** is connected to the disappearances — it stole Candlefoot's voice and was spotted by Dirlagraun when his child vanished.
- **Erophin** was crowned the Witchlight Monarch and gained the Charm of the Monarch (butterfly wings, flight, +5 charisma, 3 uses).
- The party negotiated Kettlesteam into returning Candlefoot's voice in exchange for getting her an audience with management.
- Mr. Witch's parting advice: **"Mind the rule of three."**
- Mr. Light's parting quest: **"Find the alicorn. Free the dormant queen at last."**
