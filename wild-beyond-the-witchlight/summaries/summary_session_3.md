## Characters Present

- **Pip** -- Haregon bladesinger wizard, seeker of lost rhythm
- **Erophin** -- Wood elf swarmkeeper ranger, trailed by his ever-present birds
- **Baba (Uma)** -- Tabaxi monk, carnival worker turned reluctant adventurer
- **Aruni** -- Elderly elf druid, forager extraordinaire

## Recap

Session 2 ended with the party at the threshold of the Brigand's Tollway -- Agdon Longscarf's catwalked domain blocking the route to Downfall. Session 3 opens as they step onto the creaking boards above the bog.

---

### Across the Creaking Catwalks

The mist was thick above the bog as the party crossed the Brigand's Tollway -- a network of rickety wooden walkways circling a giant tree stump. Baba, trusting feline agility more than sense, tried to vault across a gap. A botched acrobatics check later and she plunged into the muck with a wet slap, the others staring down at her in dismay. Pip lowered himself over the edge and grabbed her ankle, hauling her back up while the rest of the party tried very hard not to laugh. Baba wiped off the worst of the muck, flicked a bit onto Pip "for his skin," and pressed on.

### Enter Agdon Longscarf

Out of the mist, a figure dropped his cloak in a theatrical flourish: a six-foot-long blue scarf unfurling, a hot branding iron in one hand, a small round shield in the other. The brand was shaped like a circle of bunnies. In the distance, bagpipes struck up the same ballad the brigands had sung in Session 2 -- the ballad of **Agdon Longscarf, Brigand Prince of Prismeer**. Two skiffs slid out of the bog, each carrying haregon archers with stones and crossbows.

Battle on the catwalks. Pip cast Mage Armor, activated Bladesong, and pushed his AC to 21 before leaping over a boardwalk gap to engage. Aruni dropped a Moonbeam between the skiffs and began roasting brigands in radiant light. Baba burned her feline agility to blitz forward, flurrying strikes on Agdon -- who backflipped out of the first punch in true Pikachu fashion. Erophin popped his butterfly wings for a second time, conjured Zephyr Strike, and flew overhead to pepper the brigands with arrows.

Agdon was *fast*. Seventy feet of movement per turn, dodging past Pip and into the swarm, slamming his branding iron into whoever stood still too long. Pip dropped a natural 20 with his rapier -- a beautiful back-step slash -- but Agdon kept disengaging, flipping over his pole, and vanishing into patches of invisibility between strikes. The brigands in the skiffs started dropping to Aruni's beam and Erophin's arrows, but Agdon was everywhere and nowhere.

### Baba Goes Down

Two turns in, Agdon pivoted on Baba. His branding iron connected. *Searing fire damage.* Then again, while she was already bleeding. She went down, unconscious on the boards, a glowing bunny brand burnt into her skin.

Pip cast Sleep -- Agdon made the save by a breath, shook it off, and *kept running.* Erophin tried Fireball range, realized he didn't have Fireball. Aruni dropped Moonbeam to run to Baba, cast Cure Wounds at 2nd level (four d8s -- a beautiful roll), and Baba snapped awake with Pip shaking her shoulder. "What the fuck, Pip?" "You're amazing, Aruni."

### The Hare and the Hare-Man

Agdon went invisible and sprinted away through the reeds. The party couldn't see him. Aruni, standing over Baba, ritual-cast **Find Familiar** for the first time -- and a small hare appeared at her feet, which she sent hopping through the marsh to track the scarfed bunny. Baba caught his scent on the wind and charged. Erophin, circling overhead, drew his longbow, conjured an **Ensnaring Strike**, and loosed a single arrow. A tangle of vines erupted from the board Agdon was standing on, wrapping around his legs. He pitched forward into the mud, prone and restrained. Baba shoved him deeper. He went still.

Non-lethal. The party had him.

### A Throne for a Brigand

Rope-ladders descended from the tree stump. A few dozen curious haregon brigands watched as the party marched an unconscious, mud-slicked Agdon up to his own camp. The Tollway turned out to be a sprawling encampment built atop the stump -- patchwork tents, a carved wooden throne, and piles of stolen trinkets around it. The brigands weren't angry. Some of them looked relieved.

Baba rifled the pile. She recovered her own dinner box ("*Never touch my stuff again, stupid bunny*") and pulled out several curios:

- A rotten ogre tooth, etched with an Elvish glyph in the shape of a moon
- A tinted green monocle that turns everything you see green
- A small wooden magnifying glass
- A carved wooden wine charm shaped like a sprite -- which Baba immediately strung onto the iron key from the awakened trees, making herself a keychain

When Agdon came to, a dagger hovering politely near his throat, the party questioned him. He admitted it freely: he had a pact with **Bavlorna Blightstraw**. He stole from travelers and delivered tribute to her. No specific quotas, just *stuff*. Baba peered at him through her new green monocle (a roll of "insight with extra steps") and confirmed he wasn't lying. The brigands, watching from the edges, seemed to agree among themselves that Agdon was *weak sauce* -- and that the cats and bunnies who'd knocked him down might be better patrons. When the party asked if the clan would like a little regime change, there was quiet cheering. Agdon, outvoted, was permitted to slink away into the mist. "Bye bye, stupid rabbit."

The brigands fed the party -- carrots, truffles, root vegetables -- and gave them a patch of the stump to sleep on. The long rest passed without incident. In the morning, the clan directed them to Downfall and reminded them: the bullywugs were obsessed with etiquette. **Be polite. Be courtly. Be gorgeous.**

### Into the Mistpearl

The fog thickened as the party poled a skiff through the waterways. Somewhere in the murk, two rowboats slid alongside, crewed by elegantly dressed bullywugs in bejeweled finery. Greetings flew in Sylvan, deep bows were exchanged ("Your Froggyness," "Your Excellent Fogness"), and the newcomers directed the party via the prescribed pilgrimage route: across the **Stepping Stones**, through the **Balloon Factory**, over the **Bridge of Chattering Heads**, and thence to the **Gazebo** to present themselves before **King Gullop the Nineteenth**.

### The Stepping Stones

The stones that crossed the pond looked like ordinary boulders. Baba hopped confidently from one to the next -- and the third one *grumbled*. "**Who dares step upon me?**" It turned out one of the stones was a rock elemental named **Gallop Doer**, deeply offended to be mistaken for furniture. After a hasty apology ("I'm very sorry -- I thought you were part of the path"), Gallop Doer graciously allowed them to jump over him. Erophin, bird-brained and grinning, asked if he could knock on Gallop's back on the way past. He was politely ignored.

### The Balloon Factory

The next stop was a thatched Mickey-Mouse-shaped building with black-scorched windows, pouring smoke from its roof. Inside, an anguished bullywug was throwing buckets of water onto smoldering piles of patched fabric. Papers and designs had burned. The party pitched in, slinging water until the last embers died. The grateful bullywug introduced himself as **Duke Grint** -- "Ickrin" -- and explained that a prisoner had recently escaped Downfall, a bullywug "wiggle walk" who'd been captured in time. Someone had set animated coals loose through the factory. He suspected the escape was connected.

As Pip looked around, he recognized the patchwork cloth -- it was the same fabric as Sir Talavar's crashed balloon. This was where the bullywugs built them. This was where their faerie dragon ally had been helped to freedom.

In thanks, Duke Grint pinned a small brass brooch -- shaped like a fly with wings -- to Pip's chest. "Wear it when you present yourself. It will help."

### The Bridge of Chattering Heads

The bridge stretched over a narrow channel, lined on both sides with tall poles. On every pole: a bullywug head. Some still talking. All bickering. *"The current king is a weak man." "The one before me was better." "My successor is a fool."* The bullywugs apparently had a tradition of mounting their ousted monarchs for commentary. One head, pointedly, warned the party: *"There's another coup in the works."*

In the middle of the bridge stood a figure in a yellow straw-stuffed overcoat, topped with a pumpkin. A scarecrow. He was muttering along with the heads.

"**Clapperclaw?**"

"I've heard many great things about you," Pip tried.

The scarecrow turned -- pumpkin head revolving with a dry wooden scrape. "That is not me. My head -- my *true* head -- is the most glorious stag's skull. It was stolen. I wear this pumpkin only in protest." His voice jingled faintly; something in his straw rattled like coins.

The party had come to Clapperclaw for passage to Thither. He agreed -- *if* they could return his stag skull. He believed **Agdon** had taken it, and Agdon had likely handed it to Bavlorna. The party exchanged a look. Agdon was already in their rearview mirror, and Bavlorna's name kept leading them back to the same place. Pip asked after **Vorneal Stellhour**. Clapperclaw didn't know the name. Nobody in Downfall did.

"Don't lose your head again," Baba offered, by way of farewell. Pip winced.

### The Court of King Gullop XIX

Past the bridge, a gazebo of tarnished marble rose on a small mound, its pillars half-sunk and its canopy cracked. Cushions, harp music, and clusters of finely-dressed bullywugs lounging around a low platform. On the platform: a throne of reeds and ornamental frogs, occupied by a bullywug in a woven lily-flower crown, reading a massive leatherbound book with his lips moving. A small crocodile named **Snoodle** dozed at his feet.

"*Have you no herald to announce your presence before King Gullop the Nineteenth?*"

Baba sank into a deep double-kneed bow and improvised: "**Your Majesty, Sovereign of Lily and Reed, Keeper of the Emerald Waters, Monarch of the Gilded Pond and Royal Amphibious Highness** -- we are here because we have many questions, and we hope you can answer them."

The guards relaxed. Gullop tilted his head, charmed despite himself. Flattery, in this court, was oxygen.

The audience was less helpful than hoped. Pip asked after Vorneal Stellhour. Gullop didn't know the name. Aruni and Pip asked about Bavlorna. Gullop practically spat her name; he wanted nothing to do with her. He produced a boot from somewhere in his book and tossed it to Pip. "*Return this to her. And stay out of her way.*"

But there was a route: Bavlorna would only grant audience to members of the **Soggy Court**. To join, they would need proper attire and proper papers. They would need to visit the **Sinking Palace**. The king waved them off. Baba relayed the chattering heads' warning about a possible coup. Gullop dismissed it with a frog's disdain: "*I wouldn't necessarily take their word for anything.*"

### Toward the Sinking Palace

The party walked the thin strip of land toward the Sinking Palace -- three round sunken sections connected by a tree. As they passed a half-submerged pillar of water, Aruni stopped.

A spider's web was strung across the pillar, and in its center a king-jewel fern was folded like a secret. Enormous by any measure. A green reminder that nature -- *her* nature, the forests and ferns of her home -- still reached her even here, in this moist, ruleless place. The guard of the fern touched two fingers to her wrist: *the forest has not left you.* A small signal from a friend, through a foreign window. Aruni breathed. The party, noticing she'd paused, waited a moment.

"What is that?" Baba peered at the fern.

"It's alive," Aruni said, quietly. "Something of home."

They walked on. Along the way, Pip explained to the others that the person he was looking for was a lost teacher -- someone who had taught him to fight, then vanished eight years ago. Aruni, for her part, admitted she didn't know who she was looking for, which struck the party as a very strange way to be on a quest. "*Is it normal for druids to have quests? I never had a quest in my life.*" Pip shrugged. "I've been looking for mine for eight years." Two students. Two missing people.

The session ended at the doors of the Sinking Palace -- the next step toward joining the Soggy Court, the next step toward Bavlorna.

## Key Discoveries & Decisions

- **Agdon Longscarf** was defeated, spared, and deposed. His brigand clan no longer threatens the Tollway.
- Agdon confirmed: the brigands had a **tribute pact with Bavlorna Blightstraw**. The stolen goods went to her.
- **Duke Grint** ("Ickrin") -- a friendly bullywug at the balloon factory -- gave Pip a brass fly brooch to help at court.
- **Clapperclaw** the scarecrow will guide the party to Thither, but only if they retrieve his **stag skull** -- his true head, which Agdon apparently gave to Bavlorna.
- **King Gullop XIX** rules Downfall from the Gazebo. His pet crocodile is **Snoodle**. He hates Bavlorna but will not confront her.
- To speak with Bavlorna, the party must **join the Soggy Court** -- which requires proper attire and paperwork from the **Sinking Palace**.
- A bullywug prisoner recently escaped; someone set fires in the balloon factory as part of the escape. One of the **chattering heads** warned "*another coup is in the works.*"
- **Baba** was branded on the arm by Agdon's bunny-circle branding iron (fire damage; the glowing mark will fade, but it's visible for now).
- **Aruni** learned to cast **Find Familiar** and now travels with a hare as her familiar.
- **Items acquired**: boot for Bavlorna (from Gullop), rotten ogre tooth with Elvish moon glyph, tinted green monocle, wooden magnifying glass, carved wine-charm sprite (now a keychain with the iron key), brass fly brooch.
