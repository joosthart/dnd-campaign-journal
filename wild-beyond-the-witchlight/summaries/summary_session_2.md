# Session 2: Into the Mist

## Characters Present

- **Pip** -- Haregon bladesinger wizard, seeker of lost rhythm
- **Erophin** -- Wood elf swarmkeeper ranger, trailed by his ever-present birds
- **Baba (Uma)** -- Tabaxi monk, carnival worker turned reluctant adventurer
- **Aruni** -- Elderly elf druid, forager extraordinaire

## Recap

In Session 1, the party explored the Witchlight Carnival, uncovered a conspiracy of disappearances, stole Mr. Witch's pocket watch, and used the Hall of Illusions mirror to step through to the other side -- arriving on the crumbling edge of a massive stone bridge in a strange, misty land called Prismeer.

---

### Arrival on the Broken Bridge

The session opened with the party standing on the remnants of a colossal white stone bridge, roughly twenty feet wide and a hundred feet high, its surface glowing faintly. Enormous sections had collapsed, making a crossing impossible. Below stretched a hazy swampland reeking of standing water and rotting plants, alive with discordant birdsong and the croaking of unseen creatures. Pip felt a tingling at the base of his skull -- something in this realm called to him.

Before descending, Erophin and Aruni spotted a patchwork balloon drifting through the air in the distance, trailing a small basket before vanishing into the mist. The party noted it and began their climb down. Baba, ever the cat, descended with feline confidence. The others followed without incident, finding plenty of handholds along the weathered stone.

### The Brigand Ambush

On the swampy ground below, the party heard singing growing louder through the mist. Six haregon figures emerged, two of them leading a giant snail on a leash, the rest carrying clubs and catapults. They announced themselves with a theatrical song praising their leader -- Agdon Longscarf, the self-styled Brigand Prince of Prismeer -- boasting of his bright blue scarf and lightning speed.

Their spokesman, Jebek, cheerfully declared it a robbery. But these brigands weren't after gold. They wanted something more personal: the feeling of delight from each party member's happiest memory.

Pip recoiled -- he'd already lost memories at the carnival. Baba deadpanned that she had no happy memories at all, which genuinely seemed to unsettle the brigands. When diplomacy stalled, the party shifted to intimidation. The rolls landed hard -- 18s across the board -- and the brigands' bravado evaporated. They turned tail and vanished into the mist. Baba took credit: "Bunnies are scared of cats."

### The Crooked Tower and Sir Talavar

Navigating through the thick, shifting mist (holding a rope for safety, at Baba's nervous insistence), the party found the crooked tower -- a crumbling stone structure wrapped in dark branches, leaning at an alarming angle. Tangled in the branches at the top, about thirty feet up, hung the woven basket of the crashed balloon they'd spotted earlier.

A small, refined voice called down: "Psst! You there? I need your help!" It was **Sir Talavar**, a violet faerie dragon and loyal servant of the Summer Queen. He explained he'd been making a daring escape from Bavlorna Blightstraw when an ill wind sent his balloon crashing into the tower. His pilot, the bullywug Wigglewog, had not survived. Now Sir Talavar was trapped in a silver birdcage, and he needed to deliver urgent news to his queen.

Erophin activated the Charm of the Monarch -- sprouting colorful butterfly wings with a grumpy expression that delighted the others -- and flew up to investigate. He found the basket, the cage, and the unfortunate Wigglewog. Sir Talavar explained that the cage could only be opened with a special key held by a goblin named **Jingle Jangle**, who lived atop nearby Telamy Hill. In exchange for his freedom, he offered his sword -- dagger-sized to the party.

Erophin flew back down and relayed the information, somewhat garbled. ("Jingle Jangle is stuck in a cage!" "What? Jingle Jangle is in the cage?" "No -- Sir Tabular -- Sir Talavar -- he's the one in the cage!") The party sorted it out and set off for the hill.

### Telamy Hill and Jingle Jangle

Telamy Hill rose from the swamp like a sore thumb, its slopes covered in enormous willow trees that swayed without wind. As the party climbed, five of these awakened trees shuffled forward to greet them: "Welcome to Telamy Hill. How long are you planning to stay?"

"Short," the party promised.

The trees confirmed that Jingle Jangle lived at the top and told them she'd been attacked and robbed by haregon brigands three days ago while foraging for truffles. She hadn't left the hilltop since and was likely hungry. Sensing an opportunity, Aruni and Erophin headed into the swamp to forage. Between Aruni's wanderer instincts and Erophin's survival skills (natural 20), they returned with a generous haul of truffles in short order.

At the summit, they found a keyhole-shaped cave entrance leading down a worn stone staircase into a candlelit chamber scattered with hundreds of keys. Jingle Jangle herself appeared -- a small goblin wearing a coat made entirely of keys (hence the name), carrying a flail, and sporting a cautious smile. She was friendly but still shaken from her mugging.

The party traded the truffles for the key to Sir Talavar's cage. Jingle Jangle also shared useful intelligence: the brigands' leader Agdon Longscarf wore a bright blue scarf and moved "as fast as lightning." Their camp, the Brigand's Tollway, was built on old wooden catwalks circling a giant tree stump to the north. She also suggested seeking out **Clapperclaw**, a scarecrow who could guide them to the neighboring realm of Thither -- ruled by a hag named Granny Nightshade -- and mentioned he could usually be found near Downfall.

As the party departed, the grateful awakened trees presented them with a gift: an iron key that Jingle Jangle had dropped during her ordeal. "It might prove useful later on."

### Battle of the Serpents

Returning to the crooked tower, key in hand, the party debated how to handle the two giant constrictor snakes sleeping in the brambles near the entrance. Baba volunteered to climb but rolled poorly on acrobatics. The group pivoted to Plan B: Pip cast Speak with Animals and approached the door, hand on sword, trying diplomacy.

"Hello there. I just want to pass through the door..."

He rolled a natural 1 on persuasion. The snakes woke up angry.

The first real combat of the campaign erupted. It was a learning experience for everyone -- initiative rolls were compared, bonus actions were debated, and the finer points of concentration spells were hashed out mid-fight. But the party acquitted themselves well:

- **Pip** activated Bladesong (pushing his AC to 19), summoned a Shadow Blade, and went to work dual-wielding psychic shadow energy and his rapier, dancing around snake strikes.
- **Erophin** marked one snake with Hunter's Mark and peppered it with arrows, his swarm of birds harrying the creature and shoving it prone.
- **Aruni** dropped a Moonbeam on the battlefield, dealing necrotic damage to the snakes while simultaneously healing Baba with its radiant energy.
- **Baba** charged in with Step of the Wind, unleashing martial arts strikes and Flurry of Blows -- though she spent much of the fight grappled and constricted by one of the snakes, failing multiple escape attempts. She used Deflect Attack to completely negate a 13-damage constriction, reducing it to zero.

Erophin's birds finished the first snake in spectacular fashion -- swooping down like "the basilisk scene from Harry Potter" -- and the creature's corpse turned to stone, becoming a statue bristling with arrows. The second snake fell to a combined barrage of arrows, blades, and poison spray.

Baba emerged from the snake's coils with her ego slightly bruised but her body intact: "I still have a lot of energy left. I didn't have to do anything. Could just be in a nice hug."

### Freeing Sir Talavar

With the snakes dispatched, the party climbed the spiraling staircase inside the tower and unlocked the birdcage. Sir Talavar flew free and, true to his word, presented his sword -- a +1 dagger for the party.

He then shared what he knew: **Zybilna**, the archfey who once ruled Prismeer, had been overthrown by the **Hourglass Coven** -- three hag sisters who despise each other but cooperated long enough to splinter the realm and freeze Zybilna in time using something called the **Cauldron of Iggwilv**. Zybilna was now called "the Dormant Queen."

**Bavlorna Blightstraw**, the hag who captured Sir Talavar, lived in a spooky cottage at the heart of Downfall, a bullywug settlement. Sir Talavar warned that she could create minions that looked like tiny versions of herself, and that the bullywugs were "quite gorgeous" -- or at least, easily offended. Best to be polite.

In a private moment, Sir Talavar addressed Pip's lost rhythm. He spoke of someone proficient in the arcane who fights with swords -- a fae named **Vorneal Stellhour** (Vorneal, V-O-R-N-E-A-L; Stellhour, S-T-E-L-L) who was from Prismeer. Pip's eyes lit up. This could be a lead on his mysterious mentor.

Sir Talavar bid the party farewell with a courtly bow and flew away into the mist, off to report to the Summer Queen.

### Toward the Tollway

The party debated their next move. Downfall seemed like the destination -- it's where Bavlorna lived, where answers might be found, and where Clapperclaw the scarecrow guide could be located. The Brigand's Tollway lay between them and their goal, north of Telamy Hill.

They set off through the swamp, and as the mist parted, rickety wooden catwalks came into view -- stretching over a deep bog, supported by large tree trunks, climbing upward into the murk. The Brigand's Tollway loomed before them.

The session ended there, the party poised on the threshold of Agdon Longscarf's domain.

## Key Discoveries & Decisions

- **Zybilna (the Dormant Queen)** has been overthrown and frozen in time by the Hourglass Coven using the Cauldron of Iggwilv
- **The Hourglass Coven** consists of three hag sisters who hate each other but divided Prismeer among themselves
- **Bavlorna Blightstraw** rules this part of Prismeer from a cottage in Downfall, a bullywug settlement; she can create tiny minion copies of herself
- **Thither** is the neighboring realm, ruled by the hag Granny Nightshade
- **Agdon Longscarf** leads the haregon brigands, wears a bright blue scarf, and moves with supernatural speed
- **Clapperclaw** the scarecrow, found near Downfall, can guide the party between realms
- **Vorneal Stellhour** -- a fae bladesinger from Prismeer -- may be connected to Pip's lost mentor and rhythm
- The party obtained a **+1 dagger** (Sir Talavar's sword) and a mysterious **iron key** from the awakened trees
- The brigands tried to steal the party's happiest memories -- the same kind of emotional theft the carnival was involved in
