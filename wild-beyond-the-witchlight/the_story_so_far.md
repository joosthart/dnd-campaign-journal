*A chronicle of four lost souls, a carnival of wonders, and the misty realm beyond the looking glass.*

*Last updated after: Session 3*

---

## The Party

**Erophin** is a 150-year-old wood elf with golden eyes, green-splashed skin, and two large horns. He dresses in worn black and brown leather and carries an elegant black sword with an emerald set in the hilt, alongside a longbow. Birds flock around him constantly, as if drawn by some unseen force. He doesn't know what he lost — only that something is missing, and his birds led him to the Witchlight Carnival to find it. He was crowned the Witchlight Monarch and carries the Charm of the Monarch: the ability to sprout butterfly wings and fly, three times.

**Pip Moonstep** is a 22-year-old harengon — rabbit-folk — with light brown skin, darker hair, and pale blue eyes. He carries a short sword and fights with a dance-like bladesinging style taught to him by a mysterious faerie mentor. Eight years ago, as a young rabbit, Pip snuck into the Witchlight Carnival and followed a strange melody into a hall of mirrors. When he emerged, the rhythm that guided his magic was gone, and his mentor never returned. He came back to the carnival seeking answers — and has now followed the trail into Prismeer itself, where a fae named Vorneal Stellhour may hold the key to his past.

**Baba**, also known as Uma, is a tabaxi with purple skin and white hair. She's roughly nine years old, though her sense of time is unreliable — she doesn't quite know how long she worked at the carnival, doing odd jobs in exchange for housing. She received a free ticket from an anonymous benefactor and was the most reluctant to step through the mirror. It was Pip who convinced her, taking her hand at the threshold. What she lost remains unclear, but it may be her memories or her grip on time itself. She now carries a glowing "bunny brand" burned onto her arm — a lingering mark from Agdon Longscarf's branding iron.

**Aruni** is a 142-year-old druid with light dreadlocks and a well-used walking staff. She carries a large backpack bursting with plants and potions and prefers the company of nature to cities. She is searching for someone, but doesn't know who — only that small signs keep pointing her forward. Her birds led her to Erophin, and through him, to the carnival and beyond. As of the swamps of Hither, she has learned to cast Find Familiar and travels with a small hare familiar.

## Chapter 1: The Witchlight Carnival

The Witchlight Carnival arrives once every eight years, heralded by purple-tinted air, enormous dragonflies, fairies trailing smoke, and a low mist that crawls across the ground. Four strangers — each carrying the weight of something lost — converged on its gates on the same night.

Pip returned to the place that had taken his rhythm. Erophin followed his birds, who insisted on flying toward the carnival with unusual urgency. Aruni trailed her own flock, which led her to the wood elf they seemed so keen on. And Baba was already there, as she had been for longer than she could remember.

They explored the carnival together with the easy familiarity of people who share unspoken common ground. In the Feasting Orchard, Aruni demolished a cupcake eating contest and secretly pocketed the prize — a potion of invisibility disguised as a cupcake. On a gondola ride piloted by Feathereen, a philosophical swan, they shared their ages and their ideas about joy. In the Hall of Illusions, they rescued a halfling named Ruben from his own embarrassment after Tasha's cabinet struck his partner with hideous laughter mid-proposal. The mirrors showed each of them aged or young — Pip old and fading, Erophin grey but at peace, Baba a tiny kitten. They met Candlefoot, a monochromatic mime who had lost his voice to a raven while proposing to Palasha the mermaid.

At the Big Top Extravaganza, Pip tried to dance. His rhythm — the very thing he'd lost — betrayed him. The crowd went quiet. Baba followed with a flaming hoop leap that brought the house down. Erophin attempted archery tricks that were politely redirected by Mr. Light, the flamboyant elven ringmaster.

After the show, a bugbear named Burly led them backstage. Mr. Light and Mr. Witch — the carnival's owners — praised the group's spirit but deflected questions about the losses. "We are not responsible," Mr. Light said carefully. "Forces are at work on the other side, entirely beyond our control." Mr. Witch checked his pocket watch and added: "What is lost is lost. Gone's gone."

But Burly told a different story. Privately, he confessed that people and things had been disappearing from the carnival for years. He believed the owners knew more than they let on but that "somehow their hands are tied." He proposed a plan: steal Mr. Witch's pocket watch — the device that keeps the carnival running — and use it as leverage. The party would spread joy to earn a nomination for the Crowning of the Witchlight Monarch, creating a distraction for the heist.

They gathered allies. Candlefoot agreed to find wire snips for the watch chain. Dirlagraun, a displacer beast at the Lost Property wagon, shared his own heartbreak — his child had vanished from the carnival without a trace, and he'd seen a blackbird nearby. A boy named Viro stole Dirlagraun's most treasured possession, a mirrored ball that had been his missing child's favorite toy. Pip chased him down and returned it.

Meanwhile, eavesdropping on the owners' wagon, Baba and Erophin overheard Mr. Witch complaining about "that kenku pest." At Silversong Lake, the party caught a heckler shapeshifting from human form into a kenku — a raven-like humanoid named Kettlesteam. She was a warlock whose patron, an archfey named Zybilna, ruler of a realm called Prismeer, had gone silent. She had stolen Candlefoot's voice not out of malice but to cause enough trouble to force the owners' hand. She offered a deal: help her get an audience with management, and she would return the voice. She also revealed what she'd overheard the owners say: *"We agreed to this pact. Our hands were forced. But her eyes were open. We let the Hourglass Coven take what it wants, and in return we stay in business."*

The plan came together on the night of the Crowning. Mr. Light's golden scepter swept the crowd and settled on Erophin — the quiet do-gooder who had given a button to a monkey, rescued a halfling, and spread kindness without seeking credit. He was crowned the Witchlight Monarch, showered in fairy dust, and granted the power to sprout butterfly wings and fly. While the crowd celebrated, the invisible Baba slipped backstage, snipped the watch chain, and pocketed the prize. A natural 20 on sleight of hand. Clean as a whistle.

Confronted with the theft, Mr. Light and Mr. Witch didn't rage — they opened a door. In the Hall of Illusions, the aging mirrors revealed the owners' true reflections: younger versions of themselves, revealed as shadar-kai — shadow elves from the Shadowfell. Mr. Light explained that the land beyond the mirrors was Prismeer. Anyone who sneaks into the carnival without a ticket is robbed of something precious, and whatever is stolen always finds its way there. The mirror was a one-way portal. Getting back would be up to them.

Kettlesteam honored her deal, removing the thorn from a corn-husk doll and returning Candlefoot's voice. There were tears. There was joy.

Mr. Light taught them the password: *"Hither, thither, here and there. Wander yonder, show me where."* Before the last of them stepped through, Mr. Witch offered a cryptic warning — **"Mind the rule of three"** — and Mr. Light added a quest: **"Find the alicorn. And free the dormant queen at last."**

One by one, reflections reached out from the glass, touched them, and pulled them through. Baba first. Then Pip. Then Aruni. And finally Erophin, newly crowned and freshly winged, stepped into the mist — and into Prismeer.

## Chapter 2: Hither

The party emerged on the crumbling edge of a colossal white stone bridge, its surface glowing faintly, suspended a hundred feet above a hazy swampland. Enormous sections of the bridge had collapsed, making a crossing impossible. Below, the air stank of standing water and rot. Discordant birdsong and the croaking of unseen creatures filled the mist. Pip felt a tingling at the base of his skull — something in this realm called to him.

They spotted a patchwork balloon drifting through the air before it vanished into the murk, then climbed down the bridge into the swamp. They didn't get far before six haregon brigands emerged from the mist, singing a theatrical ballad about their leader — Agdon Longscarf, the self-styled Brigand Prince of Prismeer, who wore a bright blue scarf and moved "as fast as lightning." The brigands didn't want gold. They wanted the party's happiest memories.

Pip recoiled — he'd already lost too much. Baba deadpanned that she had no happy memories at all, which genuinely unsettled the brigands. When diplomacy stalled, the party turned to intimidation. The rolls landed hard — 18s across the board — and the brigands' bravado evaporated. They fled into the mist. Baba took credit: "Bunnies are scared of cats."

Through the shifting fog, the party found a crooked stone tower wrapped in dark branches. Tangled at its top hung the crashed basket of the balloon they'd seen earlier. A small, refined voice called down: *"Psst! You there? I need your help!"* It was Sir Talavar, a violet faerie dragon and servant of the Summer Queen, trapped in a silver birdcage. He'd been escaping from Bavlorna Blightstraw when an ill wind sent his balloon crashing into the tower, killing his pilot, the bullywug Wigglewog. He carried urgent news for his queen but could not free himself — the cage required a special key held by a goblin named Jingle Jangle, who lived on nearby Telamy Hill.

Erophin activated his monarch's charm, sprouting colorful butterfly wings with a grumpy expression that delighted the others, and flew up to investigate. He relayed the situation to the party — somewhat garbled. ("Jingle Jangle is stuck in a cage!" "What? Jingle Jangle is in the cage?" "No — Sir Tabular — Sir Talavar — he's the one in the cage!") They sorted it out and headed for the hill.

Telamy Hill rose from the swamp like a sore thumb, its slopes covered in enormous willows that swayed without wind. Five of these awakened trees shuffled forward to greet the party and told them Jingle Jangle had been attacked and robbed by brigands three days prior. She was too frightened to leave and likely hungry. Aruni and Erophin foraged truffles from the swamp and brought the haul to Jingle Jangle's keyhole-shaped cave. Inside, amid hundreds of keys and candlelight, the small goblin in a coat made entirely of keys traded the cage key for the truffles. She also shared intelligence: the brigands' camp, the Tollway, was built on wooden catwalks circling a giant tree stump to the north. And if they needed a guide to the neighboring realm of Thither, ruled by a hag called Granny Nightshade, they should seek out Clapperclaw the scarecrow near Downfall. On the way out, the grateful trees gifted the party an iron key Jingle Jangle had dropped, suggesting it might prove useful later.

Returning to the tower, key in hand, the party faced the two giant constrictor snakes sleeping in the brambles near the entrance. Erophin tried diplomacy via Speak with Animals. The snakes didn't but it. The snakes woke up angry.

The first real combat of the campaign erupted. Pip activated Bladesong and summoned a Shadow Blade, dual-wielding psychic energy and his rapier. Erophin marked a snake with Hunter's Mark and loosed arrows while his bird swarm harried the creature. Aruni dropped a Moonbeam on the battlefield. Baba charged in with Step of the Wind and Flurry of Blows but spent much of the fight grappled and constricted — though she used Deflect Attack to completely negate a 13-damage squeeze. Erophin's birds finished the first snake in spectacular fashion, its corpse turning to stone mid-death, and the second fell to a combined barrage of arrows, blades, and poison spray. Baba emerged from the coils with her ego intact: "I still have a lot of energy left. I didn't have to do anything. Could just be in a nice hug."

With the snakes dispatched, the party climbed the tower and freed Sir Talavar. True to his word, he presented his sword — a +1 dagger — and shared what he knew. Zybilna, the archfey who once ruled all of Prismeer, had been overthrown by the Hourglass Coven: three hag sisters who despise each other but cooperated long enough to splinter the realm and freeze Zybilna in time using something called the Cauldron of Iggwilv. Bavlorna Blightstraw, one of the three, ruled this part of Prismeer from a cottage at the heart of Downfall, a bullywug settlement. Sir Talavar warned that she could create tiny minion copies of herself and that the bullywugs were easily offended — best to be polite.

Before departing, Sir Talavar pulled Pip aside. He spoke of a fae named Vorneal Stellhour — someone proficient in the arcane who fought with swords, from Prismeer. Pip's eyes lit up. This could be a lead on his lost mentor.

Sir Talavar bowed and flew away into the mist.

The party set off toward Downfall, but the Brigand's Tollway lay between them and their destination. As the mist parted, rickety wooden catwalks stretched over a deep bog, climbing upward into the murk — the threshold of Agdon Longscarf's domain.

## Chapter 3: Downfall

The crossing of the Tollway began in slapstick. Baba misjudged a jump and dropped, ankle-deep, into the muck below; Pip leaned over the catwalk, grabbed her by the foot, and hauled her back up while the others struggled not to laugh. She flicked a handful of mud onto Pip for his skin, and the party pressed on.

The mist parted around a theatrical figure. Cloak flung wide, six feet of sky-blue scarf unfurling, hot branding iron in one hand and a small round shield in the other — Agdon Longscarf himself, announced by the bagpipe strain of his own theme. Two skiffs slid out of the bog carrying his brigand archers, and the ballad's promise was made flesh: he moved as fast as lightning. Seventy feet per turn, flipping out of punches, vanishing between strikes.

The fight blazed across the catwalks. Pip cast Mage Armor, entered Bladesong (AC 21), and leapt the gap to engage. Aruni dropped Moonbeam between the skiffs and began boiling brigands in radiant light. Erophin sprouted his butterfly wings for a second time, conjured Zephyr Strike, and flew overhead raining arrows. Baba spent her feline agility to close the distance and flurried into Agdon, who backflipped clear and pressed his branding iron into her ribs. The brand seared; she went down, unconscious on the boards, a glowing circle of bunnies burnt into her skin.

Pip tried Sleep. Agdon shrugged it off. Aruni dropped her Moonbeam to run to Baba, cast Cure Wounds at second level, and Baba snapped awake under Pip's shaking hand. Agdon went invisible and sprinted into the reeds. For the first time in her life, Aruni ritual-cast Find Familiar — a small hare appeared at her feet and hopped off through the swamp to track the scarfed bunny. Baba caught his scent. Erophin drew his longbow, sighted on the tracked blur, and loosed a single arrow with Ensnaring Strike. Vines erupted from the board Agdon stood on; he pitched forward, prone and restrained. Baba knocked him out.

The brigand clan watched them march an unconscious Agdon up to his own camp — a sprawling encampment of tents and looted trinkets built atop the great tree stump. When he came to, a dagger near his throat, he confessed everything easily: he had a pact with Bavlorna Blightstraw to steal from travelers and deliver tribute. Baba squinted at him through a freshly looted green monocle — a confirming glance that he spoke the truth — and retrieved her old dinner box from the pile with murder in her eyes. ("Never touch my stuff again, stupid bunny.") More trinkets came up in the sweep: a rotten ogre tooth etched with an Elvish moon glyph, a wooden magnifying glass, a carved wooden wine-charm shaped like a sprite (which Baba promptly strung onto the iron key from the awakened trees, making herself a keychain). The brigands, visibly relieved that their Prince had been dethroned, cheered quietly when the party offered new management. Agdon was permitted to slink into the mist. The clan fed the party, offered a patch of the stump to rest on, and the first long rest in Prismeer passed without incident.

In the morning, pointed toward Downfall and reminded to *be polite, be courtly, be gorgeous*, the party poled a skiff through the waterways. Somewhere in the thickening fog, two rowboats slid alongside, crewed by bullywugs in bejeweled finery and drowning in courtesies. Welcomes were exchanged in Sylvan ("Your Froggyness," "Your Excellent Fogness"), and the newcomers directed them via the prescribed pilgrimage: across the Stepping Stones, through the Balloon Factory, over the Bridge of Chattering Heads, and thence to the Gazebo of King Gullop the Nineteenth.

The stepping stones looked like boulders. The third one objected. *Who dares step upon me?* It was Gallop Doer, a rock elemental with a pedigree for being mistaken for furniture. After sheepish apologies, he consented to be jumped over.

The Balloon Factory sat at the next crossing — a thatched Mickey-Mouse of a building, windows black with soot, smoke pouring from its roof. Inside, a distressed bullywug named Duke Grint (Ickrin to friends) was throwing buckets of water onto smoldering patchwork. The party joined in until the last embers died. Grint explained: a bullywug prisoner had recently escaped Downfall, and someone had set animated coals through the factory to cover the flight. Pip recognized the patched fabric as the very same material as Sir Talavar's crashed balloon — the bullywugs had built it, and had been the ones to help the faerie dragon flee Bavlorna in the first place. In thanks for the fire line, Grint pinned a brass brooch shaped like a fly to Pip's chest. *Wear it when you present yourself. It will help.*

The Bridge of Chattering Heads stretched across a narrow channel, lined with poles. Every pole held a bullywug head. Some were still talking. All of them insulted one another loudly and without end — Downfall apparently mounted its ousted kings as commentary. One head, in parting, warned the party: *I think there's another coup in the works.* In the middle of the bridge stood a figure in a yellow straw-stuffed overcoat, topped with a pumpkin. The scarecrow. *Clapperclaw?* The pumpkin head turned with a dry wooden scrape: *This is but a poor substitute. My head — my true head — is the most glorious stag's skull. It was stolen.* Agdon had taken it, he said, and Agdon had likely given it to Bavlorna. Return it, and he would guide them to Thither. Pip asked after Vorneal Stellhour. The name meant nothing to him.

Past the bridge rose the Gazebo, a tarnished marble pavilion sunken at the knees, its canopy cracked, cushions and harp music and finely-dressed bullywugs lounging on the steps. On a reed-and-frog throne sat a bullywug in a woven lily-flower crown, reading a massive leatherbound book with his lips moving. A small crocodile named Snoodle dozed at his feet. Have you no herald, he asked without looking up, to announce your presence before King Gullop the Nineteenth? Baba sank into a double-kneed bow and improvised: *Your Majesty, Sovereign of Lily and Reed, Keeper of the Emerald Waters, Monarch of the Gilded Pond and Royal Amphibious Highness.* The guards relaxed. The king tilted his head, charmed in spite of himself. Flattery in this court was oxygen.

The audience was less helpful than hoped. Gullop had not heard of Vorneal Stellhour. He tossed Pip a stray boot he carried in his book — a gift, of a sort, for Bavlorna — and spat her name, wanting nothing to do with her. But there was a route: Bavlorna granted audience only to members of the Soggy Court, and to join, the party would need proper attire and paperwork. They would need the Sinking Palace. Baba passed along the chattering heads' warning about a coup; Gullop dismissed it with a frog's disdain.

On the walk to the Sinking Palace, Aruni paused at a half-submerged pillar strung with a king-sized spider's web, a king-jewel fern folded in its center like a secret. A green reminder that her nature — her forests and ferns — still reached her, even here. A small signal from a friend through a foreign window. The party, noticing she'd stopped, waited. As they walked on, Pip explained that the person he was hunting was a lost teacher — someone who had taught him to fight, then vanished eight years ago. Aruni admitted she still didn't know who she was looking for. *Is it normal for druids to have quests?* she asked. *I never had a quest in my life.* Pip shrugged. Two students. Two missing people. Two quests through the mist.

The session ended at the doors of the Sinking Palace.

## What We Know

### The Hourglass Coven
Three hag sisters who overthrew Zybilna and divided Prismeer among themselves. They used something called the Cauldron of Iggwilv to freeze Zybilna in time. They hate each other but cooperated for the coup. Known members:
- **Bavlorna Blightstraw** — rules Hither, the swampy realm the party is currently in. Lives in a cottage in Downfall. Can create tiny copies of herself.
- **Granny Nightshade** — rules the neighboring realm of Thither.
- A third, unnamed sister.

### Zybilna (The Dormant Queen)
The archfey who once ruled Prismeer. Frozen in time by the Hourglass Coven. Mr. Light's final instruction was to "find the alicorn and free the dormant queen at last." Kettlesteam, who had Zybilna as her warlock patron, can no longer contact her.

### The Carnival's Pact
Mr. Light and Mr. Witch are shadar-kai (shadow elves) who made a deal: the Hourglass Coven takes what it wants from carnival visitors, and in return the carnival stays in business. Anything stolen from visitors finds its way to Prismeer. Mr. Witch warned the party to "mind the rule of three."

### Key NPCs Met
- **Sir Talavar** — violet faerie dragon, servant of the Summer Queen. Freed by the party. Now carrying urgent news to his queen. The bullywugs of Downfall helped him escape Bavlorna in the first place.
- **Kettlesteam** — kenku warlock of Zybilna. Returned Candlefoot's voice. Seeking answers about her silent patron.
- **Burly** — bugbear carnival worker. Helped orchestrate the watch heist.
- **Candlefoot** — mime who lost his voice to a raven. Voice restored by Kettlesteam.
- **Dirlagraun** — displacer beast. His child vanished from the carnival. Saw a blackbird.
- **Jingle Jangle** — key-collecting goblin on Telamy Hill. Robbed by brigands.
- **Agdon Longscarf** — haregon Brigand Prince of Prismeer. Blue scarf. Lightning fast. Defeated by the party, deposed, and permitted to slink away. Had a tribute pact with Bavlorna. Likely took Clapperclaw's stag skull and gave it to her.
- **Clapperclaw** — scarecrow in yellow straw overcoat, currently wearing a pumpkin as a substitute head. His true head is a glorious stag skull, stolen by Agdon. Will guide the party to Thither if it is returned.
- **King Gullop XIX** — bullywug king of Downfall. Rules from the Gazebo in a lily-flower crown, with a pet crocodile named **Snoodle**. Hates Bavlorna but will not confront her. Granted the party a boot to return to her.
- **Duke Grint ("Ickrin")** — friendly bullywug at the balloon factory. Helped the party with information. Gave Pip a brass fly brooch for presenting at court.
- **Gallop Doer** — a rock elemental posing as a stepping stone. Easily offended but ultimately accommodating.

### Places Visited (Hither)
- **Broken Bridge** — the arrival point
- **Crooked Tower** — Sir Talavar's prison
- **Telamy Hill** — awakened willows and Jingle Jangle's key cave
- **Brigand's Tollway** — Agdon's former camp, now leaderless and friendly to the party
- **Downfall** — the bullywug settlement:
  - Stepping Stones (watch out for Gallop Doer)
  - Balloon Factory (recently set ablaze during a prisoner's escape)
  - Bridge of Chattering Heads (former kings mounted on poles)
  - The Gazebo (King Gullop XIX's court)
  - The Sinking Palace (next stop — where one joins the Soggy Court)

### The Soggy Court
Bavlorna grants audience only to members of Downfall's Soggy Court. To join, the party must obtain proper attire and paperwork from the Sinking Palace. Etiquette is everything; flattery is oxygen.
